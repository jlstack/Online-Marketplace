About

My name is Luke Stack, and I am a bit of a hobbyist. Over the years those hobbies have included soccer, guitar, photography, snowboarding, fly-fishing, welding, and my personal favorite, woodworking.

My obsession with woodworking first began in Fall 2012, when I was required to do my high school senior project. I chose to design and build a coffee table, which became my entertainment stand because of its size. I did this under the mentorship of my uncle, Roger Hamby.

Since then, I have tried to pursue woodworking to varying degrees throughout college. My sophomore year, I took a bowl turning class through Appalachian State's Craft Enrichment Program, which I would later come to teach my last semester of college. However, taking Introduction to Fabrication with Phil Armstrong was by far the best experience I've had in college. I used this class as an opportunity to build my second major piece, a red oak end table. I also took Professor Armstrong's welding class in the Fall of 2017.
